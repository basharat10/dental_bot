# Root Canal Treatment
A root canal is a procedure used to save a tooth that is badly decayed or becomes infected.

## Why it's needed
When the pulp (the soft tissue inside your tooth) becomes inflamed or infected, a root canal is necessary. This can happen due to deep decay, repeated dental procedures, or a crack in the tooth.

## The Procedure
1. **X-Ray:** To see the shape of the root canals and determine if there are any signs of infection in a surrounding bone.
2. **Anesthesia:** Local anesthesia is used to numb the area near the tooth.
3. **Pulpectomy:** An opening is made and the diseased pulp is removed.
4. **Filling:** The roots are filled with a biocompatible material, usually a rubber-like material called gutta-percha.

## Recovery Time
- Most patients return to normal activities the next day.
- Avoid chewing on the treated tooth until it's fully restored by a dentist.
- Sensitivity is normal for a few days.
